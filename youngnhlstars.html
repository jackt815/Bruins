In 2017, a year which to many would be a rebuilding year for the Bruins organization, six rookies propelled the B's to playoff success.

In order of my opinion of their potential, those rookies are:

  1. Charlie McAvoy RD #73


    Photo by Maddie Meyer/Getty Images

    McAvoy is a smooth skating defensman with excellent offensive ability. In addition, McAvoy is one of the best defensman in the league at shot suppression, and is already playing first pairing minutes as a 20 year old.

    Games Played Goals Assists Points +- TOI
    63 7 25 32 20 Avg 22:09

  2. Jake DeBrusk LW #74


    Photo by Getty Images

    DeBrusk is a skilled, hardworking winger with unlimited energy, and good vision and patience to find the right pass.

    Games Played Goals Assists Points +- Avg TOI
    70 16 27 43 19 14:22

  3. Ryan Donato C/RW #17


    Photo by Adam Richins, Boston Sports Journal

    Donato is an above-average skater with good hockey IQ, a ridiculously quick release and a powerful, accurate shot. In limited action last year, he starred for the Bruins, scoring a goal and two assists on his debut.

    Games Played Goals Assists Points +- Avg TOI
    12 5 4 9 2 14:42

  4. Danton Heinen C/LW/RW #43


    Photo by AP Photo/Paul Sancya

    Heinen is a quick skater with good passing ability, and a strong 200 foot game.

    Games Played Goals Assists Points +- Avg TOI
    77 16 31 47 10 15:01

  5. Anders Bjork LW/RW #10


    Photo by Brian Fluharty-USA TODAY Sports

    Bjork is a agile, talented two-way forward with quick hands, and excellent speed.

    Games Played Goals Assists Points +- Avg TOI
    30 4 8 12 2 12:21

  6. Matt Grzelcyk LD #48


    Photo by AP Photo/Winslow Townson

    Grzelcyk is a smaller but speedy, mobile offensive defensman with good vision.

    Games Played Goals Assists Points +- Avg TOI
    61 3 12 15 21 16:44
