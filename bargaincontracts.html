The Bruins have several talented NHL veterans locked up to long term contracts that are cheap relative to their production.

  1. Brad Marchand

    Photo by Jeremy Roenick, Twitter

    Contract when signed: 8 years, $49 Million ($6.125 million AAV)

    Remaining: 7 years

  2. Patrice Bergeron

    Photo by Getty Images

    Contract when signed: 8 years, $55 Million ($6.875 million AAV)

    Remaining: 4 years

  3. David Pastrňák

    Photo by Herald File

    Contract when signed: 6 years, $40 Million ($6.67 million AAV)

    Remaining: 5 years

Bergeron, Marchand, and Pastrnak, who together make up the best line in the NHL, are by far out-producing their contracts. Bergeron is the best two-way center in the league, and Marchand scored 85 points in 68 games last year, good for 1.32 ppg, third in the league. In addition, Pastrnak is only 22 and already producing at nearly a PPG level, with 80 points in 82 games last year.
